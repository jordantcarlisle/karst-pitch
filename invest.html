Capital Structure

Why two entities

Combining real estate and operating risk forces every investor to accept both profiles — narrowing the pool and complicating the raise.

Real Estate Co.

Acquires the property and develops generalizable improvements that serve any tenant.

  • Property acquisition ($3.5M)
  • Rooftop structure ($850K–$1.25M)
  • Mezzanine loft ($180K–$260K)
  • Parking, landscaping, infrastructure
  • Soft costs (architect, engineering, permits)

Lease

KARST OpCo

Builds out the KARST-specific facility, then operates it.

  • Cave thermal build-out ($880K–$1.1M)
  • Foundation + entrance renovation ($300K–$480K)
  • All FF&E ($456K–$858K)
  • Patio, garden, outdoor programming areas
  • Pre-opening (reserve, marketing, staffing)

The key insight

Real Estate Co. investors are underwriting a downtown Bentonville property at a 10-year hold. They fund the acquisition and generalizable structural improvements — rooftop, mezzanine, parking — that serve any tenant. KARST's success is upside, not the thesis.

KARST OpCo investors are funding the KARST-specific build-out and pre-opening. Their ~$2.6M buys the thermal infrastructure, fitness build-out, FF&E, and operating runway. The risk is execution: can KARST reach break-even by Year 3? The return on invested capital is ~50% annually at maturity.

Rent Structure

Years 1–3 · At-Cost
~$555K/yr
Covers Real Estate Co.'s carrying costs: debt service (~$485K on $5.2M at 7%/20yr), property tax, insurance, basic maintenance. KARST is not yet profitable — at-cost rent protects the operating business during ramp-up. PropCo breaks even.
Year 3+ · Premium
~$750K/yr
At-cost + ~$195K annual distribution margin. Triggered when KARST reaches break-even profitability. Enables shareholder distributions to PropCo investors.

Downside Protection

Carrying costs covered

At-cost rent covers PropCo's expenses from day one. The property is cash-flow neutral, never distressed, regardless of KARST's operating performance.

Re-tenanting optionality

Generalizable improvements — parking, rooftop, mezzanine, upgraded mechanicals — serve any commercial tenant: coworking, event venue, restaurant, retail.

Property appreciation

Downtown Bentonville within 0.5 mi of Walmart HQ on a 10-year trajectory. Estimated 2–3x appreciation on acquisition price independent of tenant performance.

Equity buildup

Even at breakeven rent, PropCo is paying down principal every month — building equity through debt amortization that compounds over the 10-year hold.

Ramsey's Reality Check — 50% Haircut

Advisor Ramsey Ball's guidance: assume real-world performance delivers ~50% of projected EBITDA. Under this stress test, KARST shifts to at-cost rent (~$555K), reducing costs by ~$195K. The dual-entity structure provides a pressure valve that the single-entity model lacks:

PropCo investors defer distributions — carrying costs still covered by at-cost rent. At stressed revenue (~$1.48M) and at-cost rent, KARST is near breakeven — the operating reserve ($250K–$500K) covers any shortfall. No investor class faces catastrophic loss.
