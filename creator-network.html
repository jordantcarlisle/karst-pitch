Strategic Brief · Creator Economy

The Network We're Already In

Evidence-based health media has moved past influencer houses. It now runs on a decentralized network of credentialed creators, corporate sponsors acting as proxy agencies, and a small set of physical Longevity Hubs. KARST sits in that third layer — whether we acknowledge it or not.

Thesis

Why this matters for KARST

The evidence-based health creator economy — Huberman, Attia, Rhonda Patrick, Thomas DeLauer, and the tier below them — is no longer just a content category. It has become a decentralized network with three infrastructure layers:

1. Academic and grassroots collectives (Harvard T.H. Chan Creator Program, The Evidence Collective) that supply scientific legitimacy.
2. Corporate sponsors (Momentous, InsideTracker) that function as de facto talent agencies, vetting creators through clinical rigor rather than follower count.
3. A small set of physical Longevity Hubs where the protocols these creators teach actually get practiced.

The Position

KARST is a Longevity Hub in that third layer. We are being built in the same category as Submersive (Austin), Clinique La Prairie's expansion hubs, and the neighborhood-scale bathhouses that have spun out of this creator economy.

The audience that already exists — millions of people reading protocol guides from these creators — is the audience looking for a place to actually do the thing. Most of them don't have one within driving distance.

The strategic question is not whether KARST is part of this network. It is: what role do we want to play in it, and what does that mean for the Canopy media studio, our partnership framework, and our revenue model?

Market Shift

From Influencer Houses to Decentralized Networks

Traditional content houses (TikTok collectives, Fenty Beauty House, Hype House) depend on physical cohabitation and shared aesthetic for virality. That model is functionally dead in evidence-based health, because credentialed creators cannot risk reputational dilution by associating with unvetted peers.

What has replaced it is a decentralized network bound together by four mechanisms that produce the effect of a shared house without any shared address:

Four Binding Mechanisms

Cross-pollination via podcasting. Attia guests on Huberman. Patrick guests on Attia. DeLauer references both. Audiences get exposed to the whole allied network.

Shared corporate sponsors acting as proxy management. Momentous alone holds contracts with Huberman, Stacy Sims, Kelly Starrett, the US Department of Defense, and 175+ pro sports teams. That roster functions like a talent agency.

Shared production infrastructure. Boutique agencies like Scicomm Media and DoubleUp produce the uniform "this looks like science" aesthetic across multiple creators.

Shared physical endpoints. This is where KARST enters. The Longevity Hubs being built in Austin, Dubai, and elsewhere are where the content network resolves into lived experience.

Creator Business Models

Four Archetypes — and What Each Means for Us

There is no single model. Four distinct monetization strategies dominate the evidence-based health creator economy. Each implies a different relationship with KARST as a physical venue.

Mega-Sponsor Network

Huberman Lab · Scicomm Media

Free-to-consume broadcast at scale. Revenue from high-ticket integrated sponsorships (AG1, Eight Sleep, Momentous). Functions like a modern TV network, with academic credentials justifying premium CPMs.

  • Primary EngineLong-form YouTube and podcast
  • MonetizationExclusive multi-million dollar sponsor contracts
  • Revenue Mix~75% sponsors, ~20% premium/merch, ~5% AdSense
  • Expansion PathSecondary properties, endorsed product lines

KARST Relevance

This is the audience-acquisition top of the funnel. A creator at this tier visiting KARST, filming at the Canopy media studio, or running a protocol session in the Cave reaches more people in a weekend than we could reach in a year of direct marketing.

Design Implication

The Canopy media studio should be built to broadcast-grade spec: black backdrop, cinematic lighting, no wellness-cliche signifiers. Scicomm Media's visual language is the reference.

Premium Subscription

Peter Attia · Early Medical

Deliberately zero sponsorships to preserve clinical objectivity. Monetizes the top 1% of the audience willing to pay premium for deep technical translation, show notes, and AMAs. Smaller reach, exceptional LTV.

  • Primary EngineDeep-dive interviews, exhaustive written show notes
  • MonetizationTiered subscriptions ($150–$2,000+/yr), books
  • Revenue Mix~80% subscriptions, ~20% books and IP
  • Expansion PathDigital courses, HNW protocol programs

KARST Relevance

The archetype our membership model should study most closely. Attia's audience is already paying $150–$2,000/yr for an intangible subscription built on trust. A KARST membership delivers the tangible counterpart.

Pricing Implication

Our membership pricing should be anchored against this willingness-to-pay, not against local gym comps. The audience overlaps. The purchase is emotionally equivalent.

High-Volume Affiliate

Thomas DeLauer · FoundMyFitness tactical tier

Shorter, high-frequency content focused on actionable tactics. Monetization comes from affiliate links and owned-brand products integrated into problem-solving narratives.

  • Primary EngineShort YouTube videos (10–20 min), high search volume
  • MonetizationAffiliate links, AdSense, own-brand coaching
  • Revenue Mix~40% affiliates, ~40% sponsors/own brand, ~20% AdSense
  • Expansion PathProprietary D2C CPG products

KARST Relevance

Less strategic as a hosting venue, but this tier drives regional traffic. DeLauer-style followers are the ones who will drive four hours to try a new facility once they see it.

Channel Implication

Treat as earned-media / pull-through, not a partnership tier. One tactical video endorsing KARST's protocols could pressure-test peak-day throughput by itself.

Corporate Ecosystem Backing

Momentous · InsideTracker

Not a creator — but the most important archetype for KARST to understand. Momentous grew 1,000%+ post-merger with Amp Human. Functions simultaneously as a product manufacturer, research grant-maker, and talent collective. Funded Voice In Sport ($500K, 2025) and Collective X ($200K, 2026).

  • StructureScientific advisory board + ambassador roster
  • Creators on RosterHuberman, Sims, Starrett, + others
  • Institutional Backing10 DoD contracts, 175+ pro sports teams
  • What They Don't Have YetA physical brand activation site

KARST Relevance

There is a plausible version of KARST where a Momentous-equivalent brand becomes a foundational partner — supplying the Cave's supplement protocols, co-funding Canopy research programming, subsidizing membership for a demographic segment.

The Unexplored Vector

This is the most interesting unexplored revenue vector in our current model. Momentous does not yet have a physical analogue. Neither does InsideTracker. There is a clear opening.

Trend One — Subscriptions Eating Sponsorships

Creators increasingly want recurring revenue they control, not cash-for-ads deals. Supercast's top 10 health creators now gross $12M+ in annual subscription revenue alone. KARST membership is the physical analogue to this shift, and we should pitch it that way.

Trend Two — Equity Is How Top-Tier Creators Now Partner

When we approach a creator about a KARST partnership, the frame should not be "appearance fee + sponsored post." It should be "equity or revenue share in the facility." That aligns incentives over the 5–10 year horizon a KARST partnership needs to make sense.

What This Means For Us

Five Tactical Implications

Concrete decisions this analysis forces us to make — now, while space programming and capital structure are still open.

Implication 01 · The Canopy

The media studio is not an amenity. It's infrastructure.

If we take the creator-network thesis seriously, the Canopy's media studio is the highest-leverage square footage in the building. It is where we earn audience.

Spec implications: broadcast-grade audio isolation (not gym-adjacent); cinematic lighting and deep black backdrop; multi-camera setup for 2–4 hour long-form recording; private entrance so visiting creators don't walk through the member floor; green room that respects the practitioner's workflow.

Implication 02 · Partnerships

We need a three-tier creator partnership framework.

Before we approach anyone, we should have a structured set of asks. Three tiers worth building out:

TIER 01

Drop-In Residency

Traveling creator uses the media studio for a weekend. Films with local practitioners. Gets promotional footage.

Low friction · high volume
TIER 02

Programming Partnership

Creator runs a protocol series at KARST. Revenue share on programming. Their audience becomes our pipeline.

Middle tier · sustained
TIER 03

Equity / Advisory

One or two creators (or Momentous-tier brands) take equity as founding partners. Attia-model commitment.

5–10 year horizon
Implication 03 · Pricing

Anchor membership pricing against premium subscriptions, not local gyms.

Attia's audience pays $150–$2,000/year for an audio subscription. Supercast's top health creators gross $12M+ combined on subscriptions alone. Our audience overlaps with theirs. KARST membership is the tangible version of that same purchase.

If we price against NWA gym comps we leave money on the table and mis-signal the category.

Implication 04 · Corporate Partnership

A Momentous-tier brand sponsorship is our largest unexplored revenue vector.

Momentous does not yet have a physical brand activation site. Neither does InsideTracker. There is a clear opening for a KARST-style facility to become that site — supplement protocols in the Cave, diagnostic screening in the Foundation, programming grants in the Canopy.

This deserves a dedicated strategic conversation with Ramsey and the founding team before we finalize the supplement/apparel/retail strategy.

Implication 05 · Cipher

Cipher's role maps cleanly onto the "silent clinical partner" pattern.

Johns Hopkins' International Arts + Mind Lab backs Submersive. The Harvard T.H. Chan Creator Program backs credentialed creators. Cipher provides KARST's protocol credibility without ever appearing as a co-brand.

This is the correct posture. It is consistent with our existing brand architecture and with what's working for other facilities in the category.
