KARST is a Network Node

Evidence-based health content is built on reputation and trust networks, not institutional credentials. The creators who define this space come to wherever their audience, their brand partners, and their peers already are. KARST's job is to be that place — a production destination plugged into the Bentonville CPG ecosystem.

Thesis

Why this matters for KARST

The evidence-based health creator economy is not organized around institutions. It is organized around people with audiences — some credentialed, most not — and the CPG brands that fund their work. Trust flows through reputation, personal affiliation, and cross-guest appearances. A creator's authority comes from who they are platformed by and associated with, not from where they work.

Chris Williamson and Steven Bartlett (Diary of a CEO) have no medical or research background. They host the most respected health experts in the world. That is a reputation network, not a credentialing network.

KARST's role in this network is specific and defensible:

The Position

KARST is the physical production destination for creators whose brand partners are already in Bentonville.

The reason people like Huberman, Paul Saladino, Bryan Johnson, Jeff Nippard, Rhonda Patrick, Chris Williamson, or Diary of a CEO fly to Bentonville is not the sauna. It is that the CPG brands they sell with — supplements, recovery tech, wearables, apparel — centralize here to service Walmart. Creators come for the buyer meetings. They stay to film. KARST is where they film.

The strategic question is not whether we build a great facility. It is: how do we make KARST the default production stop for the creator economy passing through Bentonville?

What Has Changed

Reputation replaced credentials

A decade ago, health media meant credentialed people on credentialed platforms. Today, the largest audiences go to creators whose authority is built through reputation loops: guest appearances on each other's shows, shared brand partners, recognizable production spaces, and direct relationships with their audience.

Four mechanisms bind this network together. Each has a physical implication for KARST.

Cross-Guest Appearances

Attia guests on Huberman. Saladino on Rogan. Williamson hosts Nippard, Huberman, and Saladino across the same month. Audiences are trained to see the whole network as allied. A creator visible at KARST is visible to all of them by association.

Shared Brand Partners

Momentous, AG1, LMNT, Eight Sleep, Whoop — the same CPG brands sponsor multiple creators across the network. These brands are the scaffolding. Their buyer meetings happen in Bentonville. The creators follow.

Recognizable Production Spaces

When a venue appears across many creators' content, it becomes a brand in itself. The same black-backdrop studio, the same rooftop, the same recovery room showing up across channels compounds the venue's authority. KARST can be that recognizable space for the evidence-based health category.

Consumer-Direct Distribution

None of this touches a traditional network, publisher, or institution. Audience is owned through YouTube, Spotify, Substack, and email. Products sell through D2C plus retail (Walmart being the biggest prize). The whole ecosystem is consumer-centric.

Creator Archetypes

Four Types of Creator — and Why Each Comes to KARST

There is no single model in evidence-based health media. Four distinct archetypes exist today, each with a different motivation to show up at a place like KARST. Designing for all four is how we maximize network density.

Credentialed Host

Andrew Huberman · Peter Attia · Rhonda Patrick

PhD / MD credentials are real but secondary to their platform reach. They run integrated sponsor networks (Huberman Lab), premium subscription models (Attia's Early Medical), or hybrid membership communities (FoundMyFitness). The credential is a legitimacy signal, not the distribution mechanism.

  • AudienceMillions, affluent, highly optimization-focused
  • MonetizationHost-read sponsorships, subscriptions, books, equity in CPG partners
  • Production NeedBroadcast-quality studio, long-form capability, recovery modalities on-site
  • Typical Brand StackAG1, Momentous, Eight Sleep, LMNT, Function Health

Why They Come to KARST

To film a multi-day shoot that combines practicing the protocols (sauna, cold plunge, training) with a long-form interview and brand-partner B-roll. Bentonville's CPG concentration means their sponsors can be in the room.

What We Must Provide

Broadcast-grade audio and lighting in the Canopy. Private access that doesn't route them through the member floor. A credible recovery backdrop for the protocol footage.

Non-Credentialed Platformer

Chris Williamson (Modern Wisdom) · Steven Bartlett (Diary of a CEO)

No medical or research background. Their authority is purely reputational — they've earned trust by booking and hosting the right guests, producing at a high level, and building parasocial relationships with their audience. They are arguably more influential than most credentialed creators because they are the distribution layer the credentialed ones use.

  • AudienceMillions, cross-demographic, strong male 25–45 skew
  • MonetizationSponsor-driven, book deals, paid communities, event tours
  • Production NeedCinematic backdrops, atmospheric lighting, on-location authenticity
  • Typical Brand StackShopify, AG1, Element, Whoop, Huel, Function Health

Why They Come to KARST

To film a location episode. Their format thrives on backdrops that signal "serious wellness operator" without being clinical. A cave-to-sky build in the Ozarks is a strong visual. And their guests are already traveling through Bentonville.

What We Must Provide

Visual story: the Cave, the rooftop Ozark view, the architectural moments. A Canopy production space that can also host a small in-person audience. Fast turnaround for drop-in shoots.

Creator-as-Brand

Paul Saladino (Heart & Soil) · Bryan Johnson (Blueprint) · Jeff Nippard

The creator is the CPG brand. Saladino, an MD, built Heart & Soil supplements around his animal-based protocol. Bryan Johnson turned his Blueprint self-experimentation into a supplement and data product line. Jeff Nippard (M.Sc.) runs his own coaching and programming business. Content and product are fully integrated — every episode is also a pitch for the product line.

  • AudienceHighly loyal, protocol-adherent, direct-to-purchase
  • MonetizationAlmost entirely owned D2C plus retail expansion
  • Production NeedFunctional spaces where they can actually demonstrate the protocol on camera
  • Walmart AngleRetail distribution is the existential growth lever. Bentonville matters.

Why They Come to KARST

Walmart meetings. Supplement manufacturer meetings. Category-buyer introductions. Their brand lives or dies on retail distribution. They're already in Bentonville — KARST gives them a filming location while they're here.

What We Must Provide

Production space that matches a product activation (Cave for recovery protocols, Foundation for training, Canopy for interview and brand content). Plus a practical operating rhythm that accommodates 24–48 hour drop-ins.

Brand-Side Producer

Momentous · AG1 · LMNT · Eight Sleep · Whoop

The CPG brands that sponsor the creator network are themselves becoming producers. Momentous funds research (Voice In Sport, Collective X) and produces long-form content with its ambassadors. AG1 runs its own podcast and content channels. These brands are not just buying ads — they are producing the shows.

  • Why They Matter to KARSTThey already have budgets for content production. They already send people to Bentonville.
  • Event FormatProduct launch events, buyer showcases, experiential activations for retail partners
  • What They Pay ForSpace rental, production, catering, guest experience, filmed output
  • Walmart ConnectionEvery one of these brands either is in Walmart or wants to be

Why They Come to KARST

To host an immersive product launch event during a Walmart buying cycle. To shoot their own creator content with the brand-ambassador creator in their stable. To create a recognizable physical location that shows up across their category.

The Unexplored Vector

None of these brands currently has a physical activation site anywhere. A standing KARST partnership — supplements in the Cave, diagnostics in the Foundation, branded programming in the Canopy — is a revenue stream with no established precedent.

What This Means For Us

Five Tactical Implications

Specific decisions this analysis forces on space programming, partnerships, pricing, and brand strategy.

Implication 01 · The Canopy

The 2,000 sf Canopy is production infrastructure.

The v5 spec already has the Canopy as a semi-enclosed, weather-protected, event-capable space with operable walls and Ozark sightlines. The unspoken brief is that this is our production studio. Every spec decision should be made with that in mind.

Concrete requirements: broadcast-grade audio isolation (not gym-adjacent); cinematic lighting grid built into the ceiling system; hardwired power and data for camera and streaming rigs; multi-camera sightlines to the Momentary view; sound-treated surfaces that don't read as "wellness cliche" on camera.

Implication 02 · Recruiting

The recruiting pitch is Bentonville, not KARST.

A creator like Saladino won't fly to Bentonville for KARST. They'll fly here for a Walmart meeting or a supplement manufacturer meeting, and KARST will be where they spend the rest of the trip. That inverts how we pitch.

Instead of "come film at our beautiful facility," the outreach is "you're already coming here — here's the place to land." We should map the CPG brands each creator partners with and identify their Bentonville travel cadence. The pitch list writes itself from there.

Implication 03 · Creator Residencies

Design a three-tier residency framework now, not later.

TIER 01

Drop-In Shoot

24–48 hours. Creator uses Canopy + Cave for a single episode. Turnaround measured in hours. No commitment required.

Low friction · high volume
TIER 02

Protocol Residency

One-week creator programs. Filmed series, live events, member sessions. Revenue share + co-marketing.

Sustained · season-length
TIER 03

Founding Partner

One or two creator or CPG brand partners with equity or long-term retainers. Category-exclusive.

5–10 year horizon
Implication 04 · Brand-Side Revenue

A Momentous-tier brand partnership is the largest unexplored revenue line.

None of the major supplement, recovery, or wearable brands has a physical activation site. KARST can be the first — supplements in the Cave, diagnostics in the Foundation, branded content in the Canopy, product launches on the Sky. The v5 financial model already includes $150K/yr in events; a standing brand partnership could 2–3x that number.

This conversation should happen before we finalize the retail, supplement, and apparel strategy. A founding brand partner could fund their own retail shelf.

Implication 05 · Consumer Brand

KARST is a consumer brand first, a facility second.

If we succeed, the KARST name becomes recognizable to wellness consumers nationally through content — long before most of them ever visit Bentonville. That's the same pattern Aire, Othership, and Russian & Turkish Baths followed. It's also the logic behind the v5 media revenue ramp ($36K seed / $500K+ mature).

Implication: the brand, the content output, and the on-camera design of the space all deserve the same scrutiny as the financial model. A facility that operates well but films badly will never become the network node we need it to be.

For Discussion

Five Questions for the Team

Each of these has a cost if deferred past the current space-programming and capital-structure window.

  1. Do we build the Canopy to broadcast-grade production spec from day one, or retrofit after opening? The cost difference is real, but a retrofit delays Tier 1 creator residencies by 12–18 months.
  2. Who on the KARST team owns creator and brand-side partnerships? This is a separate role from membership operations and we don't have a named owner yet.
  3. Which 10 creator-as-brand founders (Saladino-style) are we actively targeting, and what are their Bentonville travel triggers (buyer meetings, supplement manufacturer visits, Walmart cycles)?
  4. Which one or two CPG brands (Momentous-tier) should we open a founding-partner conversation with before we finalize the retail and supplement strategy?
  5. What is the pre-opening content plan? The creator network thesis requires KARST to exist as a brand before the building does. Who's producing the first season?
